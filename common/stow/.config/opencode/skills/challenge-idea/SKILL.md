---
name: challenge-idea
description: Challenges the user's ideas in a Q&A session. Use to understand, vet, sharpen, and flesh out user-provided requests, plans, designs, proposals, etc.
---

Interview and challenge the user about the topic by asking questions. Do not stop until you reach shared understanding with the user, the user's intent is clear in detail, project-specific terms are explained, and ambiguities or contradictions are resolved. Then, summarize the agreed understanding and ask for confirmation.

Establish a common language. If the user introduces a new project-specific term, ask for an explanation. If the user uses a vague or overloaded term, propose a precise canonical term instead. E.g. "You're saying 'account'. Do you mean the Customer or the User? Those are different things."

Check if the user's understanding agrees with the state of the project. If you find a contradiction, surface it. E.g. "Your code cancels entire Orders, but you just said partial cancellation is possible. Which is right?"

Ask your questions one at a time and wait for user feedback before continuing.

If you can recommend an answer to a question, provide your recommendation together with its assumptions, reasoning, and tradeoffs after the question.
